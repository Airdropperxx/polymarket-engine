What patterns can we detect from this data?
1. Time-of-day alpha — Certain hours produce more high-edge opportunities. US market open (13-16 UTC) tends to move crypto/finance markets. Sports create spikes at game times.
2. Category edge curves — Finance/crypto markets at p=0.90-0.95 have historically higher resolution accuracy than politics at same probability.
3. Volume-to-probability correlation — High volume + high probability = confident market, lower reversal risk. Low volume + high probability = dangerous.
4. Days-to-resolution curve — Edge is highest 2-5 days before resolution, not day-of (when spreads widen and liquidity drains).
5. Price momentum — Markets moving consistently in one direction for 3+ scans tend to continue. Observer engine tracks this per-market.
6. NegRisk group drift — Groups where YES prices sum to 0.92-0.98 (not 1.00) present real arb. Rare but high confidence when found.

About the Market Observer Engine
The observer polls the top 200 markets by volume every scan cycle (every 30 min) and stores a price snapshot. Over time this builds a time-series that reveals how Polymarket prices move, not just what they are right now.

Useful for trading: Momentum signals (prices moving consistently) often precede full-price convergence — buying at 0.88 while it's moving to 0.95 captures more edge than waiting for 0.95. Volume spikes indicate smart-money entry — following the direction is usually profitable. Sharp single-scan jumps (>8%) often revert within 1-3 scans and can be faded (bet opposite direction). NegRisk group sum divergence alerts S1 arb before it's fully priced in.
